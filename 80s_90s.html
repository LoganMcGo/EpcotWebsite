Future World Additions

Horizons (1983)

Replacing a postponed Space pavilion, Horizons offered a look into the future of space colonies, ocean cities, and desert farming. It became a fan-favorite with its classic ending: "Choose your future."

The Living Seas (1986)

Guests "descended" into Seabase Alpha via hydrolators. At the time, it housed the world's largest saltwater aquarium.

Wonders of Life (1989)

A pavilion sponsored by MetLife, featuring Body Wars, Cranium Command, and hands-on wellness exhibits.

The Sounds of the Future: Music of EPCOT Center

EPCOT Center's soundtrack was a symphony of hope. Composers like Bruce Broughton, Buddy Baker, and George Wilkins created sweeping scores and futuristic themes that blended orchestral grandeur with early synthesizer textures.

From the gentle pulse of Tomorrow's Child in Spaceship Earth, to the soaring energy of Energy (You Make the World Go 'Round), EPCOT's music left emotional fingerprints on every visitor.

"One little spark, of inspiration... is at the heart of all creation."

Highlight Tracks:

A Day at EPCOT Center: The Guest Experience

What was it like to step into EPCOT in 1985? The answer: unforgettable.

Visiting EPCOT Center in the 1980s was unlike any other theme park experience on Earth. Guests didn't just ride attractionsâ€”they learned, discovered, and imagined.

There were no Disney characters in the park's early years. Instead, there were ideasâ€”delivered through animatronics, giant domes, hydro-lators, and interactive exhibits.

The park's layout was overwhelming: geometric buildings, solar arrays, monorails gliding overhead. Pavilions invited visitors into immersive stories about energy, transportation, and communication.

EPCOT was slow-paced, massive, and optimistic. Guests left inspired, with a deeper appreciation for science, innovation, and culture.
